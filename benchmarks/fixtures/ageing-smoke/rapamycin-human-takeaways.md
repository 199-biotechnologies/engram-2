# Rapamycin human evidence smoke fixture

## Study context

Rapamycin inhibits mTORC1 signaling and is widely studied as an ageing-biology intervention. In human translational work, everolimus and related rapalogs have been evaluated for immune function, vaccine response, and tolerability in older adults.

## Evidence notes

A randomized trial in older adults reported that low-dose mTOR inhibition improved influenza vaccine response in humans while monitoring adverse events. The intervention was associated with immune-function changes rather than direct lifespan measurement.

Human dosing evidence remains indirect for longevity because trials usually measure immune response, safety, or disease-specific endpoints instead of mortality or lifespan. Animal studies in mice report lifespan extension with rapamycin, but human longevity efficacy is still unproven.

Key entities include rapamycin, mTORC1, everolimus, influenza vaccine response, older adults, mice, and human observational safety.
